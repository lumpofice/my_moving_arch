Metric Topology

Some companies sell their butter products by the box. Whether it be a box of 4 4oz-packages of butter, or a box of 8 2oz-packages of butter, the company sells that butter in the same size box. Conceivably, the company could sell that box as 1 16oz-package of butter or 16 1oz-packages of butter. In any event, that box represents the "space" in which 100% of the purchased butter, which has been broken down to a number of pre-sized packages, resides. A concept in mathemtics, consisting of breaking down 100% of a material contained within a given space to every possible package of some pre-defined size within that space, is what we aim to tackle below. Of course, what we will ultimately be dealing with is a space of numbers, and numbers behave differently from that of butter. So, let us emphasize again the words every and possible within the phrase:

"...breaking down 100% of a material contained within a given space to every possible package of some pre-defined size within that space..."

In math, we could break down a given space of numbers into every possible package of some predetermined size—and on a website, we can do the same with a box of butter. For instance, take our attempt at drawing a 3-dimensional block of butter:

This block of butter is rectangular in shape and has 6 sides. Let us take a look directly at the short, right-facing side of this block of butter, indicated by the large R—the large R is not meant to indicate the longer, back-facing side of this block of butter— under two different conditions:

  • i) The block of butter is in its whole form, before breaking it down to smaller packages

  • ii) The block of butter has been broken down to 4 smaller packages—which many fondly refer to as "sticks"— of butter.
